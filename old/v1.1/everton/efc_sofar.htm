The Season So Far
It was the game that put the Everton ship back on the road.
Alan Green

30th December 2000
For a split second I actually thought Everton had turned the corner. Beating Arsenal and Chelsea, to make it three wins in a row. Excellent. Since the it has been downhill all the way. It seems to me that Everton have misunderstood the saying "two steps forward one step back" and seem to be doing the exact opposite. I will now discontinue this column as a protest against Everton's miserable performances lately (including a 5-0 thrashing by Man City!).

28th October 2000
Everton have continued to have a mediocre season. But they did win their latest game against Newcastle with smoothly worked goal started by a neat back-heal from Gascoigne. It's derby day tomorrow, so lets hope Everton continue their recent good form in the Derby. My continued support of Man U is slowly bearing fruit. They just lost Anderlecht 2-1 and are now third in their Champions League group. Ha ha ha.

30th September 2000
Well so far this season Everton have collected 8 points. They have shown moments of encouragement and Gazza has certainly made a few people take notice. However a mid week loss to Bristol in the Worthington Cup (will Everton ever get further in this joke of a competition??) and losing to Ipswich today (0-3 the last time I checked) have also shown how weak Everton still are and that their squad is still very shallow. In the mean time I think I'll support Man Utd when they are in Europe. It worked this week - they lost 3-1 to Eindhoven. Ha ha.

The New Season
I hope to give my opinions a bit more frequently than last season, but have to admit this is unlikely and is directly linked to the number points Everton get each week. So check back regularly, you might even be surprised.

17th July 2000
Ermm, ok this is hardly a frequent update, but then I do have an excuse. I have been busy moving countries and now live in that wonderful patch of concrete known as London. Makes a change to Darmstadt in Germany though. Anyway, Everton finished the season in 11th position (I think) after a slightly disappointing end to the season. The break saw Nick Barmby regain his place in the England team and then reward the manager who showed faith in him and gave him the run in the first team he needed to rebuild his confidence, by asking to for a transfer to Liverpool!!! Well I wish him all the best in the reserves, which is about as far as he'll get there. John Collins and Don Hutchison have also left. In return Everton have signed a couple of Scandinavians, Watson and that most consistent of performers, Gazza. Oh dear! At least things can only get better..... unless they decide to bring injury free Duncan back to Goodison. They did! Arrgghhh!!!!!

3rd September 1999
Well so far, OK. A good draw with Man U at home. A slip up at Villa and an unlucky performance against Spurs. Then two great wins vs Southampton and Wimbledon had me thinking this could be good season. But a loss away to Derby confirmed that there is still a lot of work to be done. Hopefully we won't have to endure another relegation battle.

10th October 1999
In the past month Everton have beaten both Sheffield Wednesday and Liverpool away, as well as drawing with Coventry at home and are now 6th in the league. Not bad. The win against Liverpool has probably made most supporters season already. Let's hope the team doesn't suffer from vertigo and keep the good form going. If so, maybe we can even start to dream of a place in Europe next season - UEFA Cup of course.
