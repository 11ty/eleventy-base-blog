The Directive

Introduction

On this site you will find information about the Web Accessibility Directive, also known as the WAD. The WAD is a piece of legislation of the European Union that requires some websites to be accessible to persons with disabilities. The websites it applies to are those belonging to the public sector, such the government, local councils or the police.

Your profile

The information presented on this site is organised by the different roles involved in creating and using websites in the public sector. I am looking for information as a:

  • policy expert
  • website owner
  • accessibility expert
  • web designer, developer, author
  • user
  • persons with a disability

Policy Expert

Have to know the details of the directive, implmenting decisions on statements, standards and especially monitoring. Have to have an overview of the accessibility compliance of all the websites under you responsibility. This could be all the public sector websites of your country. Have to ensuring you have adequate monitoring in place to provide you this overview.

Website owner

You are responsible for ensuring that the website you own or a responsible meets all the current accessibility legislation and guidance. You are responsible for ensuring your designers and developers have the correct knowledge and training to build accessible websites. You are responsible for ensuring your website is evaluated regularly for accessibility and that any new or modified content remains accessible.

Accessibility Expert

You will fully understand the needs of persons with disabilities and how they can be catered for. You will evaluate websites against common, agreed standards and legislation. You will report to the website owner and sign off that the reports you provide represent status of the website's accessibility compliance. You will give guidance on how to fix issues that you found during the evaluations.

Web designer, developer or author

Whilst you might be near the bottom of the list, ultimately it is the design and coding choices you make that will ensure the accessibility of the website. Ultimately it is the choices you make that determine if we live in an inclusive society. You therefore have real power over the lives of others, especially those with particular needs. User it wisely.

User

Not really for you. If you have a disability or are in some way dealing with persons with disability see below.

Website owner

You have rights to be able to access public sector information in the same way as other users. You have rights to complain if this is not the case.
